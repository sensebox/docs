# General Steps

If you have any issues collecting data from your senseBox device, follow these troubleshooting steps:
 - Enable automatic updates for the senseBox:bike app to ensure you're always using the latest version.
 - Restart the senseBox:bike. Press the power button to turn the device off, wait a few seconds, then press it again to turn it on.
 - Restart your phone and open the senseBox:bike app.

If none of these steps help, please email us at [kontakt@reedu.de](mailto:kontakt@reedu.de). When contacting support, please provide the following information:
 - Your phone's operating system and version. For Android, also include your phone's make and model.
 - The version of the app you are using (senseBox:bike app > Settings > About).
 - A detailed description of the issue, including when you first noticed it.

# Known issues and their solutions

<details>
    <summary>App cannot connect to senseBox</summary>
    - Ensure the senseBox:bike is powered on and fully charged.
    - Make sure Bluetooth is enabled on your phone and the app has permission to use it.

    If you still cannot connect to the box and you have an iPhone, please take the following steps:
    - Install [nRF Connect](https://apps.apple.com/de/app/nrf-connect-for-mobile/id1054362403).
    - Open nRF Connect, find the senseBox, connect to it, then disconnect.

    You should then be able to see the box in the senseBox:bike app again.
</details>

<details>
    <summary>Not all sensor data is being recorded (there are gaps in the data)</summary>
    - Make sure battery saver mode is disabled on your phone.
    - Ensure only the senseBox:bike is connected to your phone via Bluetooth.
    - Start recording, take a short ride, and then stop.
    - Check that you have the same number of data points for all sensors (e.g. speed and temperature).
</details>

<details>
    <summary>The app records tracks locally, but does not upload them to openSenseMap</summary>
    Workaround: You can export the recorded track as a CSV file in an openSenseMap-compatible format and upload it manually to the [openSenseMap website](https://opensensemap.org/).

    - Restart the app and check that real-time sensor data is being displayed.
    - Ensure your internet connection is stable and the senseBox:bike is close to your phone.
    - Disable battery saver mode on your phone.
    - Make sure you're logged into your openSenseMap account in the app and a senseBox is selected.
    - Start recording and move a few meters.
    - Log in to openSenseMap and check that the new data shows up in your account.
</details>
